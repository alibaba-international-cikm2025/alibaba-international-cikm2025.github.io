CIKM 2025 Alibaba International Competition Workshop

Multilingual E-commerce Product Search Competition: "Multilingual Query-Category and Query-Item Relevance"

Nov 14, 2025 • Seoul, South Korea

Introduction

This workshop highlights the results of the Alibaba International CIKM 2025 Analyticup competition on multilingual search relevance. The competition aims to advance multilingual search technologies by leveraging LLMs to improve search accuracy in noisy, real-world e-commerce environments. More specifically, two tasks are being explored:

  1. Multilingual Query-Category Relevance Task: Determine the relevance between a user's search query and a specific product category.
  2. Multilingual Query-Item Relevance Task: Assess the alignment between a user's multilingual search query and a specific product listing.

Participating teams built models on our curated dataset and submitted technical reports describing their approaches and results.

Technical Reports from Competing Teams

Below are the submitted technical reports. Click a title to view the PDF.

DragonAI: Locale-aware Session Encoder with Contrastive Pretraining

Team members: Mei Lin (lead), Xue Wang, Arjun Patel

Abstract: A session encoder that fuses locale embeddings with behavioral co-visitation graphs and contrastive objectives to improve cross-locale ranking.

SilkRoad: Transfer Learning for Low-Resource Locale Recommendation

Team members: Jing Zhao, Rohit Kumar, Elena Petrova

Abstract: A transfer-learning pipeline adapting high-resource models to underrepresented locales via meta-learning and label smoothing.

RedPanda: Hybrid Title Generation with Retrieval-Augmented Generation

Team members: Hao Li, Sara Gomez, Lucas Tan

Abstract: Combines retrieval of candidate product tokens and a lightweight seq2seq model to create succinct titles for cold-start items.

EastBridge: Two-stage Retrieve-and-Rerank with Multilingual Embeddings

Team members: Victor Chao, Mei Chen

Abstract: A scalable two-stage pipeline that uses approximate nearest-neighbor retrieval and a cross-lingual reranker.

CloudNine: Ensembling and Robust Post-processing for Leaderboard Stability

Team members: Ananya Rao, Michael Stein, Yifan Zhou

Abstract: A practical study on ensembles and deterministic post-processing to reduce evaluation variance across locales.

Instructions for Writing Technical Reports

  1. Format: Submit a PDF using the ACM SIGCONF two-column template. Recommended templates:
    • ACM proceedings template
    • You may also use the CIKM 2025 workshop-specific template if provided in the competition repository.
  2. Length:
    • Standard submissions (1–2 tasks): up to 4 pages (including figures, excluding references).
    • Comprehensive submissions (3 tasks): up to 6 pages (excluding references).
    • References and an optional reproducibility appendix (max 1 page) are allowed beyond these limits — keep the appendix focused on code, data splits, and hyperparameters.
  3. Authorship & Blindness:
    • Submissions are single-blind (author names and affiliations should be included).
    • If you are reusing a model/codebase, cite it clearly and describe additional contributions.
  4. Content to include:
    • Abstract and short introduction clarifying the task(s) addressed.
    • Data processing and splits used (train/val/test).
    • Model architecture and training details (hyperparameters, compute resources).
    • Evaluation metrics and results (with ablation and error analysis).
    • Limitations and ethical considerations (privacy, bias, etc.).
    • Link to code and checkpoints (strongly encouraged for reproducibility).
  5. Submission process:
    • Upload a single PDF file to the submission page (link to be announced on the official competition portal).
    • Make sure the PDF includes contact email for the corresponding author.
    • Winners and top-N leaderboard teams will be invited to present (in-person or virtually).
